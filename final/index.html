The ACA and Pediatric Mental Health Care

by Zach Shapiro

Maryland Ahead of the Curve in Insuring Children

Before the Affordable Care Act became law, 8 percent of children in U.S. were uninsured. For Maryland the number was 5 percent. Since 2014, when the Essential Health Benefits "benchmark" plan was implemented, the rate of uninsured children dropped to 5 percent in the U.S. and 4 percent in Maryland.

Although left in state's hands, the EHB set standards for 10 services, including mental and preventative health, which expanded coverage for pediatric care.

Not Enough Psychiatrists

The ACA's progress of insuring more children has also highlighted the disparity between those in need of care and avialable specialists. The crisis is nothing new but it hasn't improved much since the ACA was signed.

The map below, which looks at first glance like an image Donald Trump would hang in every room and pass out to guests, is actually a chart showing the number of licenced psychiatrists per 100,000 children in 2012, state by state. The data was collected by Milbrank Memorial Fund.

If you zoom in close to Maryland, you'll see D.C. is the only place on the map in the green, which means more than 47 licensed psychiatrists per 100,000 children.
