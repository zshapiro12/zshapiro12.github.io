Top 250 by Decade

Every decade had its share of classics, but IMDB was particularly impressed with cinema at the turn of the century. Ridley Scott's "Gladiator" kicked off a strong 10 years and "Up" closed it well, becoming the first animated movie to be nominated for Best Original Film at the 2009 Academy Awards.

Winning Over Viewers and Critics, Equally

So much for "One for you, one for me."

These eight directors made multiple films that not only brought in big bucks but also landed on the IMDB Top 250. Disney/Pixar in particular showed that it's possible to attrack a wide audience, mainly kids, and still make a quality film. Spielberg leads the pack with five films in both categories.

Star Wars Movies

Box office numbers don't always reflect the quality of the film, but in the case of Star Wars it's pretty accurate. The original trilogy – "A New Hope," "Return of the Jedi" and "The Empire Stikes Back" – all pulled in over $8 million while receiving at least an eight rating from IMDB. The contrast with the prequels is stark.

From a quality standpoint, George Lucas barely redeemed himself after "The Phantom Menace" but the lost interest and faith from viewers was clear. "Attack of the Clones" and "Revenge of the Sith" scored a 6.5 and 7.6, respectively, but dropped nearly $200 million at the box office.
