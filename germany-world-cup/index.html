Germany's Run to World Cup Victory

by ZACH SHAPIRO

The German men's national soccer team did not lose a single game on the road to winning the 2014 World Cup, their fourth title. This story -- told through graphics -- documents their path to victory.

Germany Got Better as Tournament Progressed

Germany tied only one game en route to winning the World Cup -- a surprise result against Ghana -- but they did face several scares. They required extra-time to beat Algeria in the quarterfinals and took 120 minutes to defeat Argentina in the final.

Stadiums Hosting Wins

Germany played seven games in six different stadiums en route to winning their fourth World Cup in 2014, traveling hundreds of miles across Brazil. They won six games and drew one, 2-2 against Ghana on June 21.

How Germany Won

Germany won the World Cup by controlling the ball with more crisp passes than any other team in the tournament, taking more high percentage shots and scoring buckets of goals while keeping their opponents from finding the back of the net.

Germany moved the ball around on offense more than any other team, by a lot. With roughly 726 passes per game, Germany out-passed the second most prolific team, Spain, by nearly 36.

Although Germany wasn’t as efficient passing the ball as Italy, who completed 85 percent compared to 82, the eventual champions sustained their success over four more games. Italy was knocked out by Uruguay in the Round of 32.

Among the four teams that played seven games – Germany, Brazil, Argentina, Netherlands – Brazil edged out Germany in average shots per game and attempts on target with 15.9 and 10.3, respectively. Germany averaged 14 shots a contest and sent 10.1 to the net, second in both categories for the Top Four.

Perhaps no stat shows Germany’s dominance in the 2014 World Cup better than the difference between goals for and goals against per game. Just nine teams were in the plus margin, and just one, Germany, hit an even two.

Common knowledge would suggest that the team with the more scoring opportunities “inside the area” would have the most success – but that wasn’t the case in 2014. Germany averaged 2.9 shots in the zone per game, ranking 32 of 32 in the category.

As the old adage goes, defense wins championships. A physical team, Germany won 13 tackles per game, and while that ranks 9th, only Brazil (13.6) played as many games among the top eight.

A Passing Machine

Germany moved the ball around the field at a rapid clip throughout the tournament, passing the ball an average of 726 times per game -- well above the median of 513 passes for all 32 teams.

Impresive on Offense, Stingy on Defense

It may seem obvious, but one reason Germany won the World Cup is that it scored more goals per game, on average, than any other team in the tournament, while letting in fewer goals per game on average than all but one team, Costa Rica.
